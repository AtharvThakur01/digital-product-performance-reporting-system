# Reporting Framework

## Purpose

This system gives digital product and service stakeholders a consistent view of product performance, operational quality, dashboard reporting, and weekly action ownership.

## Metric Dictionary

| Metric | Definition | Reporting Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adoption rate | Active users completing at least one core action in the period | Product usage trend |
| Task success rate | Percent of users completing a target workflow without abandonment | Journey quality |
| Open defects | Active QA or production issues impacting users | Release readiness |
| SLA attainment | Percent of time service availability met target | Operational reliability |
| NPS | Customer sentiment signal from product feedback | Experience quality |
| Incidents | Production incidents in the reporting period | Risk management |

## Weekly Reporting Flow

1. Refresh product analytics, QA tracker, incident log, and support data.
2. Validate metric definitions, source freshness, and missing fields.
3. Compare current performance against prior period and target.
4. Summarize drivers, risks, owners, and decisions for stakeholders.
5. Archive notes so leadership can review trend history.
